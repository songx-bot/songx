Trade Information

Import Procedures

General Import Requirements

  • Valid Import License (where applicable)
  • Proforma Invoice
  • Bill of Lading/Air Waybill
  • Customs Declaration Form
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Other product-specific certificates

Step-by-Step Import Process

  1. Obtain necessary import documentation
  2. Submit documents to customs
  3. Pay required duties and taxes
  4. Clear goods from port
  5. Transport goods to final destination

Export Procedures

General Export Requirements

  • Valid Export License (where applicable)
  • Commercial Invoice
  • Packing List
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Phytosanitary Certificate (for agricultural products)

Step-by-Step Export Process

  1. Obtain necessary export documentation
  2. Package and label goods appropriately
  3. Submit documents to customs
  4. Pay required fees
  5. Load goods for shipment

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to import goods into Ghana?

The basic documents required include a valid import license (where applicable), proforma invoice, bill of lading/air waybill, customs declaration form, and certificate of origin. Additional documents may be required depending on the product type.

How long does the customs clearance process take?

The standard clearance process takes 2-5 working days for compliant shipments, provided all required documents are submitted correctly and duties are paid promptly.

Where can I check current tariff rates?

Current tariff rates are available in our downloadable resources section or at any Ghana Revenue Authority customs office.
